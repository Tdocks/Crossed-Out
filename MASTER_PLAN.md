# Crossed Out — Master Product & Business Plan (v2)
### Scripture for Real Life
_Canonical product plan. Preserved verbatim-in-substance from the founding strategy sessions (Jul 2026). Companion: DESIGN_LANGUAGE.md (visual system), GAP_ANALYSIS_AND_ROADMAP.md (build order)._

## Product vision
Crossed Out is a personalized Christian formation, community, entertainment, outreach, and local-church platform that helps people move closer to God in ways that feel relevant to their actual lives. Central belief: **God did not cross you out.** It is not "another modern Bible app" — YouVersion owns the general Bible-reader category (1B+ installs, free). The opportunity is a connected journey no competitor completes: **personal need → relevant Scripture → biblical understanding → reflection → action → community → local church → service → outreach.** Positioning: "The app that helps you discover what God's Word says about what you're actually going through — and then helps you live it out with other people."

## 1. Brand meaning
Name supports a whole vocabulary: *God didn't cross you out · You're not crossed off · Cross off today's devotional · Cross out fear/shame/isolation · Cross the bridge toward faith/salvation/community · What are you crossing out today? · Your past does not cross out your purpose.* Balance two meanings: the cross as center of Christianity, and crossing out what no longer defines you — hopeful/restorative, never judgmental. Tagline: **Crossed Out — Scripture for Real Life.** Campaign line: *God didn't cross you out.*

## 2. Primary product promise
"Tell us what you are facing, and Crossed Out will help you discover what Scripture says, understand it in context, take a practical next step, and connect with people who can walk with you."

## 3. Personalized Scripture Engine (the core)
Users must never feel they got a random verse. Onboarding captures focus areas (anxiety, depression, motivation, discipline, purpose, loneliness, grief, relationships, marriage, parenting, forgiveness, temptation, addiction, financial wisdom, career, leadership, confidence, anger, understanding God, returning to faith, beginning Christianity, learning to pray, Bible literacy — multi-select + ranked). Also asks: what you need most; comfort vs instruction vs challenge vs balanced; Bible familiarity; time available; when you engage; tone; excluded topics; preferred translation.

Daily check-in ("What are you carrying today?": peaceful, anxious, discouraged, motivated, angry, lonely, confused, grateful, tempted, overwhelmed, hopeful, grieving). The engine combines long-term goals + current mood + recent content + saved verses + completed devotionals + feedback + tone + biblical themes. Feedback loop: This spoke to me / I needed this / not for today / I already know this / show deeper / show more practical / less like this / more like this.

## 4. Deterministic personalization BEFORE AI (cost control)
Layered architecture so expensive AI isn't called on every interaction:
- **Layer 1 — Tagged Scripture DB:** each passage tagged with topic, emotional state, spiritual maturity, tone, book, testament, person/story, context, life situation, difficulty, tradition sensitivity, notification type, related devotional/prayer/action/music/message/group.
- **Layer 2 — Rules & scoring:** deterministic relevance score (e.g., current focus +30, mood +25, requested encouragement +20, not recently shown +15, preferred translation +10, saved similar +10, disliked similar −30, shown within 30 days −20). Serves most free users with no AI call.
- **Layer 3 — Pre-generated content:** editorially-approved verse explanations, devotionals, reflection questions, prayers, actions, context summaries, outreach templates — one approved piece serves thousands.
- **Layer 4 — Cached AI answers:** approved library for common questions (anxiety, forgiveness, anger at God, money).
- **Layer 5 — Live AI:** reserved for genuine personalization (custom journey, complex passage, personal prayer, thoughtful message, comparing interpretations, weekly reflection).

## 5. Kyra (the AI guide)
Reuse the Kyra personality from other projects, adapted for a Christian context: warm, intelligent, calm, nonjudgmental, encouraging, grounded, honest about uncertainty, confident without speaking for God. Roles: Scripture guide, reading companion, prayer-writing assistant, journey planner, outreach assistant, church-discovery guide, reflection coach, community navigator. **Kyra must never** claim God told her something, know God's private will, declare an event a divine sign, tell someone to stop treatment, tell someone to stay in abuse, present a disputed interpretation as settled, or replace a pastor/counselor/physician/emergency resource. Preferred framing: "This passage may help because…", "Many Christians understand this to mean…", "Christian traditions differ on this." Initial model **GPT-5.6 Luna** via an API layer; architecture must be **model-independent**. Emphasis on relevance — nothing turns users off more than random, impersonal verses. Guard AI usage carefully: free members using AI eat cost without revenue, so build strong deterministic logic alongside AI so nothing is 100% AI-reliant.

## 6. Today experience
Center of the app. A session: check-in → personalized Scripture → context/explanation → audio → reflection question → suggested prayer → one practical action → optional music → optional sermon/video → optional share/community action. Session lengths: 2-min reset / 5-min daily / 10-min devotional / 20-min deeper study. Practical actions: send encouragement, forgive privately, gratitude list, review an expense, pray for someone by name, reconnect with someone, attend an online service, save a church, join a discussion, volunteer.

## 7. Streaks & spiritual rhythm
Keep BOTH a traditional streak (current/longest, monthly calendar, milestones — tracks Scripture engagement, devotional, check-in, listening, journey session) AND a broader **spiritual rhythm** (Scripture, prayer, reflection, community, encouragement, service, rest, church). **Grace Days** protect momentum (earned + auto-granted monthly, free tier, auto-used unless disabled; grace-not-guilt messaging). Leaderboards are small-group/cooperative only (group journey completion, collective reading days, prayers supported, encouragements sent, volunteer hours, mission funds) — never public rankings implying spiritual superiority.

## 8. Christian entertainment transition
Help users move from secular to Christ-centered content without shame. Connect Apple Music / Spotify / YouTube Music; identify genres; recommend Christian artists/playlists matched to existing taste ("You like melodic hip-hop — try these Christian artists"). Transition modes: a few recs / mix in / gradual / mostly Christian / Christian only. Movies & TV discovery layer (Christian films, faith series, documentaries, family-friendly, biblical dramas, kids) with filters (genre, service, rating, Christian intensity, family-friendliness, tradition, tone, content warnings). Never assume a user is ready to abandon all secular content.

## 9. Attend church online
Dedicated online-church experience. Crossed Out **finds churches that stream and aggregates them** so users pick without searching. Directory: live now / starting soon / Sunday AM / Sunday PM / Saturday / midweek / recorded / relevant to you / near you / popular in your community. Filters: denomination (non-denom, Catholic, Orthodox, Baptist, Methodist, Pentecostal/charismatic, Reformed, Lutheran), contemporary/traditional, teaching/worship-focused, young-adult, family, multicultural, size, sermon style, service length, music style, language, location, live chat, accessibility, kids, women's/men's ministry. Personalized church matching (transparent; never "objectively best"). Service page: church, speaker, sermon title, start time, live/recorded, duration, worship style, Scripture refs, beliefs, location, visit-in-person, save, ask a question, join the church's group, events. Online→in-person bridge after repeat attendance (parking, dress, times, newcomer contact, kids check-in, accessibility, "meet me at the door", add to calendar). Online participation is never treated as inferior.

## 10. Bible experience
Full-text reading, search, highlights, bookmarks, notes, collections, reading history, translation comparison, audio, cross-references, reading plans, memory verses, sharing, AI explanations, context summaries, related sermons/music/discussions. **Translation strategy:** start with legally/financially feasible — KJV + a suitable public-domain/open modern translation; ESV where permitted; **NIV after obtaining commercial license** (not free for unrestricted app use — confirm rights, API terms, attribution, storage/display rules). Architecture must add translations without modifying the reader. Founder wants at minimum KJV + NIV (ESV acceptable) beyond the launch public-domain set.

## 11. Cross the Bridge outreach (a signature differentiator)
Marketed as helping a friend **"cross the bridge"** toward faith. Instead of sending only a verse, the user builds a personal package: (1) why I thought of you, (2) a personal message, (3) a relevant Scripture, (4) what it means to me, (5) optional audio/video, (6) optional invitation, (7) low-pressure response. Kyra assists composing compassionate, non-manipulative messages by situation (grief, breakup, stress, anger at Christianity, curiosity, loneliness, financial pressure, nervous about church). Recipient opens a **beautiful web experience with no install**: read, listen, view Scripture in context, ask a private question, respond, request prayer, explore a beginner journey, watch a short video, attend an online service, find a nearby church, or decline — never auto-added to marketing. Shared journeys (Seven Days of Hope, Who Is Jesus?, Questions About Christianity, Finding Peace, What Christians Believe, Returning to Faith, Learning to Pray) can be done together, recipient via web before signup.

## 12. Community
Purpose-based, not follower-based. Spaces: friends, private prayer circles, church groups, young adults, new Christians, men's/women's ministry, marriage, parenting, grief, financial stewardship, fitness/discipline, Scripture study, mission teams, volunteer teams. Post types: prayer request, answered prayer, testimony, question, Scripture insight, reflection, encouragement request, event invitation, service opportunity, mission update. **Safety requirements:** unsolicited DMs off by default, strong minor protections, verified pastors/leaders, report+block, automated risk detection, human moderation escalation, no unverified fundraising links, crisis-resource escalation, private prayer requests by default, rules around medical/legal/financial advice, restrictions on claims of divine authority over another user.

## 13. Local churches, events, ministries
Local tab: churches, Bible studies, worship nights, conferences, retreats, volunteer opportunities, Christian businesses, men's/women's/youth events, recovery ministries, mission trips. **Church profiles** go beyond a map listing: statement of faith, denomination, ministry/worship/teaching style, service duration, dress expectations, parking, kids programs, young-adult ministry, men's/women's groups, languages, accessibility, livestreams, upcoming events, newcomer contact, verification status. Newcomer feature: **"I'm thinking of visiting"** → parking guidance, a welcome contact, someone to meet them, kids check-in help, accessibility support, a private question answered.

## 14. Mission trips & service
Discovery + coordination platform. Mission listings (with stronger safeguards than normal events): sponsoring org, verification, purpose, dates, location, cost, fundraising, included expenses, age, skills, medical, background checks, safeguarding, application process, trip leader, refund terms, emergency info. Start as verified discovery/application — do NOT process large trip payments initially. Service projects: feeding homeless, clothing drives, disaster response, school supplies, foster-care support, senior assistance, community cleanup, prison ministry, international aid, mission scholarships. Users volunteer, donate, share, track, get updates, view impact.

## 15. Donation platform
Trusted donation + impact system for company projects (feeding homeless, sponsoring trips, etc.). Categories: community projects, mission scholarships, homeless outreach, food distribution, disaster relief, local church projects, partner ministries, emergency needs. Options: one-time, monthly, round-up, sponsor a project, sponsor a participant, group goal, corporate match, anonymous. Transparency per project: owner, verification, goal, raised, use of funds, admin costs, updates, photos/results, completion report, refund/reallocation policy. Process via an established payment provider; review tax, charitable-solicitation, nonprofit, and fundraising requirements before launch.

## 16. Video & content platform
Scripture explanations, devotionals, sermon clips, testimonies, motivation, Bible history, difficult questions, prayer videos, worship clips, mission stories, event highlights, church livestreams. **Purposeful feed** (not infinite doom-scroll): daily curated collection, natural stopping point, save Scripture, begin a devotional, join a discussion, watch full sermon, take an action. Every theological video links to Scripture ref, speaker, church/org, tradition, review status, related content.

## 17. Automated content pipeline
Goal: operationally scalable and as **hands-off** as possible (founder runs many projects; will recruit VAs). Ingest approved church livestreams, YouTube, podcasts, sermon libraries, blogs, creators, music, event calendars, church sites → detect, transcribe, identify Scripture refs, summarize, tag, identify speaker/church, clip, caption, vertical formats, draft social posts, recommend journeys, submit for review, schedule. No AI-generated theological content auto-publishes without checks early on. **Viral Bible-video pipeline** (future): pick topic → approved passage → reviewed context → draft script → voice narration → visuals → subtitles → render formats → human approval → publish + repurpose to TikTok/Reels/Shorts → track retention/conversion.

## 18. Automated church discovery & verification
Risk-based, not manual. Stage 1 discovery (mapping providers, search, public/denominational directories, church sites, streaming platforms, socials). Stage 2 enrichment (address, site, times, denomination, livestream links, socials, programs, contact, statement of faith, events, leadership). Stage 3 confidence scoring (domain consistency, matching address/phone, active presence, directory presence, livestream consistency, contact-email domain, org age, reviews, claimed). Stage 4 church claim (email-domain, phone, DNS/site code, docs, denominational confirmation, manual when needed). Stage 5 verification levels (discovered → info confirmed → claimed → Crossed Out verified → trusted partner; always shown to users). Stage 6 continuous monitoring (broken livestreams, changed times, dead sites, leadership changes, closures, new events, suspicious edits, user reports). Only exceptions/high-risk need humans.

## 19. Automated marketing
One long-form sermon/devotional → clips, quote/verse graphics, carousels, email devotionals, push, blog articles, community prompts, journey material, podcast clips, outreach resources. Automate scheduling, hook/thumbnail testing, install-source tracking, theme identification, campaign variations, retargeting, re-engagement, challenge recommendations, church referral pages, partner reports, newsletters. VAs handle church outreach, verification exceptions, creator comms, content review, moderation, event approval, partner onboarding, support, donation updates. Founder reviews strategy, major partnerships, financials, safety, brand, roadmap, high-risk theological decisions.

## 20. Business model (freemium)
**Free tier:** Bible reading, search, highlights, bookmarks, notes, personalized daily Scripture, traditional streak, grace days, spiritual rhythm, basic audio, widgets, basic journeys, friend circles, church discovery, online-service directory, event discovery, Bridge Shares, limited live AI, approved AI answers, music/entertainment recs, donation/volunteer access. **Crossed Out Plus:** unlimited personalized AI, custom journeys, advanced Bible study, premium guided audio, deep reflection history, journaling insights, family accounts, offline content, premium creator series, memory-verse training, extra personalization, expanded media discovery, weekly Kyra review. **Cost controls:** free users get deterministic recs + cached answers + limited AI credits + smaller context + lower-cost routing; premium gets higher limits/longer conversations/advanced personalization. Never make basic spiritual support feel inaccessible. **Church subscriptions:** free basic listing; verified profile; livestream placement; event publishing; group management; newcomer inbox; content hosting; analytics; volunteer workflows; custom journeys; mission listings; member comms. (Indicative church tiers: free verified / ~$49 small / ~$149 growing / ~$399+ multi-campus.) Marketplace revenue later: event ticketing, sponsorships, mission processing, donations, creator subscriptions, church media services. Avoid ads in the Bible reader or prayer experience. **Consumer pricing guidance:** ~$5.99–8.99/mo or ~$49.99–69.99/yr; annual-first; never weekly (weekly subs tank ratings for AI-Bible apps).

## 21. Navigation
Five primary tabs: **Today** (personalized Scripture, check-in, devotional, prayer, action, music, streak/rhythm) · **Bible** (reader, search, audio, notes, highlights, bookmarks, study tools, Kyra explanations) · **Community** (friends, groups, prayer, journeys, testimonies, discussions, Bridge Shares) · **Attend** (live services, upcoming, saved churches, online sermons, church matching, in-person visit planning) · **Explore** (local churches, events, missions, service, music, movies/TV, videos, partners, donations). Profile holds progress, journal, settings, subscription, saved content, privacy.

## 22. Phased launch (from the plan)
- **Phase 1 — Personalized faith companion:** onboarding, focus engine, Today, Bible reader, notes/bookmarks/highlights, deterministic rec engine, limited Kyra, traditional streak, grace days, rhythm, personalized notifications, widgets, Bridge Shares, basic friend circles.
- **Phase 2 — Attend & entertainment:** church livestream directory, filters, matching, online service experience, music-platform connections, Christian music recs, movie/TV discovery, curated video feed.
- **Phase 3 — Local community:** church claiming, verification, local events, verified leaders, church groups, newcomer visit coordination, volunteer opportunities, creator uploads.
- **Phase 4 — Service & impact:** mission-trip directory, project donations, mission sponsorships, partner benefits, impact tracking, fundraising, service-hour tracking.
- **Phase 5 — Scaled automation:** automated church ingestion, verification scoring, content-repurposing pipeline, AI video creation, cross-platform marketing, partner onboarding, moderation triage, VA operations dashboard.

## 23. Partnerships & member benefits
Vetted partners (Christian counseling, marriage resources, men's/women's ministries, bookstores, family services, adoption/foster, financial coaching, mission orgs, local cafés/restaurants, fitness, apparel, conferences/retreats). Two benefit types: **community** (discounts, free events, priority registration, sponsored journeys, scholarships) and **impact** ("donates one meal per redemption," "supports a mission scholarship," "supports foster families"). Sponsored content clearly labeled; must not feel like a religious Groupon. Local-business discounts (e.g., 5% for members) as the community grows.

## 24. Primary competitive advantage & position
Advantage is not more Bible content — it's connecting Scripture to what the user is feeling, trying to change, already enjoys, who they care about, which church fits, which service is on now, which community supports them, which action is next. Users should feel "this was selected for me," not "today's random verse." Final position: **A personalized Christian life platform that helps people discover relevant Scripture, form lasting habits, transition toward Christ-centered content, attend church online or locally, build meaningful community, serve others, and help friends cross the bridge toward faith.** Scripture-first; everything routes back to understanding Scripture, relationship with God, application, healthy relationships, community, and service.

## Risks (from the plan)
Denominational conflict (state foundational beliefs; label tradition-specific content) · community safety (treat as core product) · empty-community problem (launch city/network-by-network) · scope creep (each area is its own product — stay phased) · spiritual credibility (one hallucinated verse / reckless AI answer / predatory leader / monetization scandal can break trust) · name perception (test that it reads redemption, not rejection).

## Viability (from the plan)
Market demand 9/10 · competition intensity 10/10 · differentiation potential 8/10 · technical complexity 9/10 · monetization 8/10 · **overall 8.3/10** — viable IF it avoids being a generic Bible reader and initially focuses on one transformative daily loop.
